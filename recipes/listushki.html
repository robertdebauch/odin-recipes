Listushki

What is Listhuski and how to make it?

You can try leaflets among the southern Komi. Their whole secret is in the "pan" made from a cabbage leaf. You put a leaf with dough in the oven and take out a loaf. Housewives in Komi villages baked "tup√∂s nyan" - hearth bread. And they put a cabbage leaf under the dough as a frying pan - hence the Russian name "leaflets". The leaf burns, they throw it away, and the bread stays clean. And not just clean, but patterned: when baked, it gets a hint of flavor, and cabbage juice, and most importantly - beauty.

In addition to flour, its base is milk, kefir or sour milk of your choice, and even better - whey from homemade cottage cheese. We add yeast, sugar, salt, vegetable or butter, if you want it tastier, and wheat flour. Mix, put in a warm place, knead, add a little flour. And you can bake.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Milk, kefir, whey or sour milk
  • Dry yeast
  • Sugar
  • Vegetable oil or butter
  • Cabbage leaves (Use large leaves).

Step-by-step cooking recipe

  1. Mix all ingredients.
  2. Knead the dough well.
  3. Leave in a warm place to rise.
  4. Place in small portions on cabbage leaves.
  5. Bake in the oven.
