Shoma Cabbage

What is Shoma Cabbage and how to make it?

A traditional dish for the end of the harvest. At the first frosts, villages and towns would light fires, put large vats on the fire and boil cabbage, which would then be salted for the winter. In different regions of Komi, this dish format has its own characteristics. Somewhere, cabbage is boiled in heads, somewhere, it is divided into halves or quarters. Anise or wild herbs are added. We will share the recipe known to us.

Ingredients

  • Cabbage
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Spices

Step-by-step cooking recipe

  1. Boil water in a vat (or stewpot).
  2. Peel the cabbage from the outer leaves. Divide into quarters (depending on the size of the head of cabbage and the vat) and put into boiling water.
  3. After 5-7 minutes, add salt and any spices (anise, cloves, allspice, dried herbs such as currant leaf or bay leaf). Cook until done.
  4. Throw the cabbage onto a pre-prepared dish.
  5. Pour unrefined sunflower oil over it, you can add crushed lingonberries and start eating.
  6. Separately, put the prepared heads of cabbage into a barrel, layering berries and currant leaves.
  7. Put under pressure and send to a cold place.

As needed, take out the cabbage and use it in cooking. You can add potatoes or mushrooms to the chopped cabbage.
