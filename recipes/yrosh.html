Yrosh (Kvass)

What is Yrosh and how to make it?

A tasty and filling refreshing drink. Oat kvass has not only a thirst-quenching effect, but also real benefits. Whole and unpeeled grains fill the finished drink with incredible strength and benefits. Carefully select grains for kvass and do not allow rancind or moldy ones to get in.

Ingredients

  • Water
  • Oats
  • Sugar or Honey
  • Raisin

Step-by-step cooking recipe

  1. Wash a glass of oats and put them on the bottom of a three-liter jar. Add two tablespoons of granulated sugar (or honey). Fill with clean water (not boiled). Put pre-soaked raisins (3-4 pcs) into the jar. Cover with gauze and leave for 3-4 days.
  2. Drain the primary kvass. Fill the starter with clean water, add two tablespoons of sugar (or honey) and four pieces of raisins. Cover the jar with gauze and let the kvass sit for four days.
  3. After fermentation, pour the kvass into bottles, and fill the starter with water and add raisins and sugar for a new batch.

Drink the kvass chilled.
