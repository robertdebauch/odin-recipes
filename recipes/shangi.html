Shan'gi

What is Shan'gi and how to make it?

Shan'ga is a dish of Russian, Mari, Udmurt and Komi cuisine. It is a product made from unleavened or yeast, wheat, rye or rye-wheat dough. The dish has spread from Karelia to the Ob, including the Russian North. Currently, it is typical for the regions of the Urals, Middle Urals and Trans-Urals.

To prepare it in Komi style, you need rye dough, filling and love. From the dough, we make molds with sides, similar to a miniature Victoria Amazonica water lily. We fill it with fillings: potato, cabbage, cottage cheese, berry, cereal or salted mushrooms. We wait until the wood in the oven burns out, rake the coals to the sides and put the baking sheets in the oven. Five minutes - and ruddy, delicious shangi appear on the shovel from the oven.

Ingredients

  • Rye flour
  • Sour milk or kefir
  • Egg
  • Salt
  • For the filling:
    • Cabbage
    • Potatoes
    • Barley or wheat groats
    • Cottage cheese
    • Lingonberries or blueberries
    • Salted mushrooms

Step-by-step cooking recipe

  1. Dough:
    1. Mix rye flour with sour milk or kefir, egg, add salt to taste.
    2. Knead well. The dough should be flexible and stiff enough not to stick to your hands.
  2. Fillings:
    • Cabbage. Pre-fry chopped cabbage in a frying pan.
    • Potatoes. Mash boiled potatoes with the water they were boiled in.
    • Cereals. Boil cereal until half-cooked or fully cooked. Salt to taste.
    • Cottage cheese. Mix cottage cheese with sugar and egg.
    • Mushrooms and berries. You can put berries and chopped salted mushrooms in molds without preliminary preparation.
  3. Baking:
    1. Mold even round molds.
    2. Put the filling in them.
    3. Grease with sour cream beaten with egg on top.
    4. Bake in a burnt-out oven for 5-7 minutes
